Research Project - Monitoring Glacier Dynamics

Monitoring Glacier Dynamics Using Geospatial Tools

In my research project titled "Monitoring Glacier Dynamics Using Geospatial Tools: A Case of Hunza Valley, Northern Pakistan," I explored the critical role of glaciers as freshwater reserves, particularly in northern Pakistan, which hosts some of the world's largest midlatitude glaciers. This study specifically focuses on the dynamics of key glaciers in the Hunza Valley, including Batura, Passu, Ghulkin, and Gulmit glaciers. Using temporal Landsat and Sentinel satellite imagery spanning from 2014 to 2024, I analyzed glacier behavior, such as advancements, retreats, and stability, within the Karakoram ranges, which are known for the "Karakoram anomaly."

To delineate glacier outlines, I employed the Normalized Difference Snow Index (NDSI) combined with slope gradient analysis, followed by meticulous manual editing for precision. Validation of the derived glacier inventories was performed using high-resolution WorldView-2 satellite imagery, ensuring the reliability of the results. Temporal analysis revealed consistent glacier retreat patterns across the selected glaciers. The observed changes in glacier extent were linked to fluctuations in annual mean monthly temperature and precipitation records.

This study not only enhances the understanding of glacial dynamics but also contributes to addressing the broader challenges posed by climate change in mountainous regions. By combining geospatial tools with remote sensing data, this research provides valuable insights into glacier behavior, which is critical for water resource management and climate change adaptation strategies.
